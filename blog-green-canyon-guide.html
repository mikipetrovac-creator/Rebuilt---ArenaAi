Green Canyon Boat Trip from Alanya: Emerald Water, Swimming & What to Pack

Updated: July 2026 · View the tour →

July 2026 · 5 min read

Hidden in the Taurus Mountains above Manavgat, Green Canyon is a vast emerald-green lake ringed by steep cliffs and pine forest — and one of the very few canyons in Turkey you can explore by boat. If you're staying in Alanya or Side and wondering what the trip is actually like, how cold the water is and whether it's worth it, here's an honest guide.

What is Green Canyon, and why is the water so green?

Green Canyon isn't a dry gorge — it's a long, deep reservoir created by the Oymapınar Dam on the Manavgat River, a great arch dam about 185 metres high and one of the tallest in Turkey. Behind it, the valley filled with water fed by dozens of cold mountain springs. That spring water is so clear and so intensely green that locals call the area Turkey's "jade kingdom". Steep limestone cliffs rise straight out of the lake, and in places the canyon narrows dramatically — which is exactly where the boat takes you.

What happens on the boat trip?

After a comfortable morning pickup from your hotel, you reach the pier and board the boat between 10:00 and 10:30. The cruise lasts around 2 to 2.5 hours: you glide across the open lake and then deep into the Big Canyon, where the cliffs close in on both sides. Along the way there's a swimming break in the emerald water — the highlight for many guests. After the cruise you drive to a lakeside restaurant at the Green Lake for lunch, with free time afterwards to swim again or try a little fishing. On the way back there's a photo stop by the Oymapınar Dam and a short stop at the Dikmen textile outlet before the return to your hotel.

How cold is the water — and can you really swim?

Yes, you can swim, and most people do. Because the lake is fed by cold underground springs, the water stays refreshingly cool even in the middle of summer — noticeably colder than the sea, which is exactly why it feels so good in the July and August heat. It takes a moment to get used to, but the water is beautifully clear. You get a swimming break during the cruise and more free time at Green Lake, so bring a swimsuit and a towel and you'll have two chances to cool off.

What to pack, and is it worth it?

Bring a swimsuit and towel, sunscreen, a hat and sunglasses, a bottle of water and a little extra money for drinks and souvenirs. Flip-flops and a dry change of clothes are handy after swimming, and a camera or phone is a must — the scenery is unforgettable. As for whether it's worth it: Green Canyon is much closer than long trips like Pamukkale or Cappadocia, so it's a relaxed day rather than an exhausting one. If you like nature, calm water and a gentle pace, it's one of the most rewarding easy day trips on this stretch of coast.

How far is Green Canyon from Alanya?

Green Canyon (the Oymapınar reservoir near Manavgat) is much closer than most day trips from Alanya. Pickup is at a comfortable morning hour and you board the boat between 10:00 and 10:30, so it's a relaxed day rather than an exhausting one.

Is Green Canyon worth it?

If you like nature, emerald water and a calm day on a boat, yes. It's one of the very few canyons in Turkey you can explore by boat, with a swimming break, lunch by the lake and a relaxed pace — a great change from the busy coast.
