Demre, Myra & Kekova: The Real Santa Claus, Lycian Tombs & a Sunken City

July 2026 · 5 min read

This full-day trip along Turkey's Lycian coast packs three extraordinary sights into one day: the church of the real Santa Claus, ancient tombs carved into cliffs, and a boat cruise over a city that sank beneath the sea. Here's what awaits you.

The real Santa Claus: St Nicholas Church in Demre

Long before the modern legend, Saint Nicholas was a real 4th-century bishop of Myra, famous for his secret gifts and kindness to the poor — the inspiration behind Santa Claus. His church still stands in Demre, with beautiful frescoes and ancient mosaics, and draws visitors from all over the world who come to discover the story behind the world's most beloved gift-giver.

Myra: Lycian rock tombs & a Roman theatre

A short drive away, the ancient city of Myra reveals rows of Lycian tombs carved high into the sheer cliffs, alongside a remarkably well-preserved Roman theatre. Standing beneath these house-like tombs cut into the rock is a striking reminder of just how advanced the Lycian civilisation was thousands of years ago.

Kekova: cruising over the sunken city

The highlight for many is the boat cruise over Kekova, where the ruins of an ancient Lycian settlement lie submerged just beneath crystal-clear turquoise water — staircases, walls and foundations visible from the boat. The site itself is protected, so you admire it from the water, and the cruise includes a swim stop in a beautiful bay where you can cool off in the Mediterranean.

Frequently asked questions

Is St Nicholas really Santa Claus?

Yes — the modern Santa Claus is inspired by Saint Nicholas, a real 4th-century bishop of Myra (today's Demre) known for his generosity and secret gift-giving. His church in Demre can still be visited.

Can you swim on the Demre–Kekova tour?

Yes. The boat cruise over the sunken city of Kekova includes a swim stop in a turquoise bay, so bring your swimsuit and a towel.

Can you walk around the sunken city of Kekova?

No — the sunken ruins are protected, so you view them from the boat as you cruise over the clear water. It's a unique way to see an ancient city beneath the surface.

Is the tour suitable for children?

Yes. Children aged 4–7 receive a discounted price and infants aged 0–3 travel free on a parent's lap. The boat cruise and ancient sites are enjoyable for all ages.
